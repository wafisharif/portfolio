Development Notes & Features

Below is a summary of the major engineering updates I implemented while iterating on the Smart Desk Assistant. Each subsystem has been refined to improve robustness, responsiveness, and real-world usability.

Enhanced Lighting Detection

The LED indicator system now uses external digital LEDs on the breadboard instead of the Arduino’s built-in LED. This provides stronger brightness and a more consistent signal. A hysteresis-based threshold was added to prevent flickering and ensure stable low-light detection.

Ultrasonic Presence Tracking

The ultrasonic module now includes a dual-threshold hysteresis buffer, allowing the assistant to reliably distinguish between a user and background objects such as a chair. This prevents false triggers when I lean back, shift position, or temporarily move out-of-range.

Closed-Loop Cooling System

A PN2222 transistor controls a small DC cooling fan, creating a closed-loop temperature regulation system. When the workspace temperature rises above 27°C and I am present at the desk, the fan automatically activates to maintain a comfortable environment.

Productivity Timer Integration

Three physical buttons—pause, reset, and skip—were wired into the system to support full control over a productivity session timer. The LCD display dynamically updates the timer status and no longer shows drifting or unstable behavior after pauses or resets.
